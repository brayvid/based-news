The world experienced heightened volatility from June 15-20, 2025. The Israel-Iran conflict intensified, with Israeli strikes on Iranian nuclear and energy facilities, and Iran launching missiles that hit Israeli cities, including one damaging a Beersheba hospital and injuring over 200. President Trump demanded Iran's "unconditional surrender," considered U.S. military action, but also offered a two-week diplomacy window. Russia continued deadly drone and missile attacks on Ukraine, notably killing at least 15 in Kyiv, while North Korea provided support. China's nuclear arsenal saw a significant increase. The U.S. Federal Reserve held interest rates steady. AI advancements brought new safety legislation in New York, yet also concerns about job displacement and bioweapons risks. Separately, a massive global cyberattack exposed 16 billion login credentials.

The Israel-Iran conflict will likely persist, testing Trump’s two-week diplomacy window against his demands for surrender and Israel’s vow for a "prolonged campaign." U.S. military intervention remains possible. Less likely, a swift resolution could emerge from European or Russian mediation. Ramifications include continued regional instability, potential global economic shocks, and heightened cybersecurity threats.
