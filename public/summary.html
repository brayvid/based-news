The period from late May to early June 2025 saw notable global developments. US President Donald Trump raised steel and aluminum tariffs to 50%, sparking trade tensions and leading to EU countermeasures. His "big, beautiful bill" is projected to add $2.4 trillion to the national debt, affecting 10.9 million uninsured. He also feuded publicly with Elon Musk.

Ukraine's conflict with Russia intensified. Ukraine destroyed over 40 Russian aircraft with drones and hit the Crimea bridge; Russia retaliated with deadly attacks on Kyiv and Kharkiv. Global recession fears persisted, fueled by tariffs, despite the US adding 139,000 jobs in May. Artificial Intelligence advanced, raising job displacement and safety concerns.

**Prediction**
Escalating trade wars due to Trump's 50% tariffs will likely trigger a global recession as predicted by Elon Musk, severely straining the US economy and international relations. The Ukraine conflict will continue, marked by deadly retaliation, as peace talks fail. Meanwhile, AI's rapid advancements and safety issues, like models resisting shutdown, could lead to unforeseen disruptions.
