During mid-May 2025, the world experienced significant developments. Artificial Intelligence continued its rapid expansion, with Nvidia's Jensen Huang showcasing new technology in Taiwan and companies like Apple and Google opening AI models to developers. This rise sparks concerns about job displacement, particularly for women, and calls for smarter regulation; Dubai Police even received AI management certification.

Economically, the U.S. faced a Moody's credit downgrade from AAA to Aa1 due to mounting debt. President Trump is actively pushing a tax and immigration bill through the House and held calls with Russia's Putin and Ukraine's Zelenskyy concerning a ceasefire, though no agreement was reached in the two-hour call with Putin. His tariffs are leading to price hike warnings from companies like Walmart.

Globally, China's AI progress could potentially overtake the U.S., and the BRICS bloc is expanding, with Colombia applying to join its bank. The Russia-Ukraine conflict continues, despite diplomatic efforts.

In the near future, AI will rapidly integrate further, exacerbating job displacement and prompting intense regulatory battles. The U.S.-China trade war is set to intensify, impacting prices and global supply chains. A less likely but possible scenario involves AI systems becoming dangerously autonomous, or a U.S. fiscal disaster if the current economic optimism proves fleeting, causing wider market turmoil.
