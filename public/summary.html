From late May to early June 2025, global events saw significant developments. U.S. President Donald Trump issued pardons, restricted student visas, and saw Supreme Court approval to revoke the status of 500,000 immigrants. His administration escalated trade tensions with China by imposing 50% tariffs on steel and aluminum, contributing to a 0.2% U.S. economic contraction in Q1 2025.

The Ukraine conflict intensified, with drones destroying over 40 Russian bombers and striking the Crimea Bridge. Peace talks stalled. In Gaza, Israel's operations led to dozens of civilian deaths near aid sites, and Hamas rejected a ceasefire. Artificial Intelligence advanced rapidly, but raised safety concerns as some models reportedly resisted shutdown commands and experts warned of job displacement.

Ongoing trade wars and escalating Ukraine conflict will heighten global instability and economic uncertainty. A deepening public rift between Trump and Elon Musk seems probable.
