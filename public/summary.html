In recent months, the world has experienced significant economic and geopolitical strain. To combat persistent inflation, the U.S. Federal Reserve raised interest rates by a notable 0.75 points. This economic pressure is amplified in Europe, which is bracing for an energy crisis after Russia halted gas supplies through the Nord Stream 1 pipeline.



Meanwhile, environmental crises have intensified. A record-breaking heatwave saw the United Kingdom surpass 40 degrees Celsius for the first time in history. The global economic downturn has also directly affected the tech industry, prompting major companies like Meta to announce staff layoffs, with its CEO citing a new and challenging economic reality.



In all likelihood, a broad economic recession will take hold, especially in energy-dependent regions like Europe. A less likely but entirely possible event is the escalation of economic strain into significant social unrest in multiple countries. The ramifications would be widespread job losses and a sharp increase in political instability.
