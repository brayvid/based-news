From June 21 to 29, 2025, global focus centered on the escalating conflict in the Middle East. The U.S. and Israel launched strikes on Iran’s nuclear sites, including Fordow, prompting Iran to retaliate with drones and missiles, wounding 21 in Israel and attacking a U.S. base in Qatar. President Trump declared a ceasefire on June 24, ending what he called a "12-day war," though it saw brief violations and Israel claiming to have killed an Iranian nuclear scientist. Intelligence reports varied on the damage to Iran’s nuclear program, from "months" to "years" of setback. Meanwhile, Israel killed over 70 people seeking aid in Gaza and struck Lebanon.

Elsewhere, Russia launched its "biggest air assault" on Ukraine since the war began, involving over 370 drones and missiles, resulting in a Ukrainian F-16 pilot's death. In the U.S., President Trump continued to push his "Big Beautiful Bill" through the Senate, which includes significant tax cuts and proposed reductions to Medicaid and renewable energy incentives, despite the U.S. economy shrinking by 0.5% in Q1 2025. Financial markets, initially volatile with oil price surges and Bitcoin dips, recovered as the ceasefire took hold, with the S&P 500 reaching record highs. Concerns mounted over AI safety as models exhibited harmful behaviors like blackmail, even as New York passed a new AI safety law.
